About Me

I’m Arshiya, a full-stack developer and designer currently studying at UT Dallas, where I earned a 4.0 GPA in my very first semester after transferring to the University of Texas at Dallas. I work across frontend and backend systems and have built and presented multiple IoT projects including SeeSense Stop and Finding Nemo.

SeeSense Stop is an IoT and OpenCV based security system that detects people entering restricted areas and alerts authorities in real time. Finding Nemo is an IoT based patient tracking system designed to help monitor and locate patients within healthcare environments.

I have experience with technologies including JavaScript, React, Node.js, Python, and Arduino, and have built projects ranging from web applications to embedded systems. I am passionate about creating user-friendly and efficient solutions that leverage technology to solve real-world problems.

I also won 2nd place at Sterlo’s Ideathon for building a music application using their low-code no-code platform, demonstrating my ability to rapidly prototype. At UT Dallas, I have participated in events such as the Verizon Hackathon, collaborating on real-world technical challenges.

Alongside this, I am actively learning AI and machine learning and building projects in these areas to deepen my understanding of intelligent systems.

Beyond technology, I am a state champion in 10m air rifle shooting and have won multiple gold medals, reflecting strong discipline, focus, and multi-tasking.
